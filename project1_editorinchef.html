Editor-In-Chef

Responsive Web APP Case study

Role: Product Research | UX Analysis | UX Design | Name Development | Brand Design | UI Design

Research analysis & UX Design:

Competitive Overview:

In analyzing two competing web apps, there seems to be a lack of humanized type interaction. How can we have an instant where the user substitutes an ingredient and then the app updates the cooking instructions as soon as they confirm the substitution? Chefs are able to think on their feet when things don’t go as planned with a recipe. This kind of advice would be great to integrate. It will give the user more confidence in using a recipe based on what they have in their kitchen.

Marketing Profile:

    • Cross promoting sites with sponsors, contributors, and members sharing recipes.
    • Awards and recognitions to help boost exposure.
    • A large social media following with engaging posts.
    • A strong and current presence of press coverage promoting the site’s mission.
    • Some competitors use ads on search engines.

SWOT Analysis:

After sorting through the Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, and Threats, some questions came up for developing the user experience:

    • What unique items can make the recipes easy to follow and understand?
    • Categorize by ingredients in their kitchen?
    • Showcase nutritional values and disadvantages?
    • Respond to user’s wants and needs to keep them focused and motivated?

UX Analysis and solutions:

    • Plug-in the main ingredients they have in the kitchen and then it displays the top recipes by category.
    • Ability to separate the ingredients user has and then display the items that are missing and show possible substitutions if missing.
    • Adjust cooking instructions as items are substituted (example: cooking scallions versus onions).

User Psychology Analysis:

    Hicks Law: Number and Clarity of Options
    Creating Trust: Transparency and Judging Book Cover
    Conditioning & Addiction: Operant Conditioning
    Gamification: Engagement Loop

Conduct User Research through the RESEARCH LEARNING SPIRAL:

Develop objectives, formulate a hypothesis, conduct interviews, and synthesize the information provided.

Key Findings:

    • Patterns: User agree about helpful ingredient substitutions. Proper layout with many visuals and legible text. A one-stop shop so they won’t have to go outside of app to get information about items.
    • Surprises: Nutritional values not as important as originally thought. Users often combines multiple recipes to experiment with flavors and are not afraid of complex recipes.
    • Frustrations: Too many ads or too much copy with a story attached to the recipe. Worthless functions.
    • Needs or Goals: Learn and expand as a cook. Ability to save and organize recipes in categories or folders. Necessary to have more knowledge and confidence when substituting ingredients.
